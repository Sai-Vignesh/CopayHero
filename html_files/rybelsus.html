Rybelsus FAQs

Medically reviewed by Karen Berger, Pharm.D.
Licensed Pharmacist

How much does Rybelsus cost without insurance?

The typical cost of Rybelsus without health insurance is $1,332.19 per 30, 7MG Tablet though you can get a discount using a SingleCare Rybelsus coupon to pay just $845.09 for 30, 7mg Tablet. You can still use SingleCare for free Rybelsus discounts if you don't have insurance. 

How much does Rybelsus cost with insurance?

Every specific insurance plan is unique and may or may not cover Rybelsus. The cost of Rybelsus with insurance coverage differs according to your plan - contact your insurance company for explicit prescription coverage and payment specifics. If your insurance plan does not cover Rybelsus, you can use a SingleCare card to get a better rate on Rybelsus at your participating local pharmacy. Also, you can use SingleCare discounts if you have Medicare or Medicaid prescription plans. If Medicare or Medicaid don’t cover your Rybelsus prescription, try SingleCare instead. Patients can use the SingleCare website or app to review the lowest cost of Rybelsus with SingleCare versus your cost with insurance or Medicare.

Other ways to save on Rybelsus 

In addition to Rybelsus coupons, there may be Rybelsus coupons and patient assistance plans available from Novo Nordisk or other organizations. However, these programs may have eligibility criteria. Rather, you can try the SingleCare Rybelsus savings card to unlock a fair cost of $845.09 for 30, 7mg Tablet. All United States or Puerto Rico citizens may use free Rybelsus discounts from SingleCare as long as you have a valid prescription.

What is the generic of Rybelsus?

Rybelsus is a trademarked prescription medication. It contains semaglutide and is made by Novo Nordisk and approved by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration. There is no generic version of Rybelsus. Use a SingleCare discount card to pay $845.09 for 30, 7mg Tablet.

What is Rybelsus used for?

Rybelsus for diabetes is used with diet and exercise to control blood sugar levels. It can be used in patients with Type 2 diabetes but is not approved for use in patients with Type 1 diabetes. Rybelsus works in several ways. This medicine stimulates insulin production and inhibits glucagon production when blood sugar increases. Which helps control blood sugar levels. This medication also slows stomach emptying, which also helps lower blood sugar. 

What are the side effects of Rybelsus?

Common Rybelsus side effects include:

  • Stomach problems like nausea, vomiting, diarrhea or constipation, stomach pain, burping, indigestion, bloating, reflux, and gas

  • Diabetic retinopathy

  • Decreased appetite

Serious allergic reactions are rare but require emergency medical attention. Symptoms may include hives, difficulty breathing, and swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat. Other serious side effects may include but are not limited to, kidney, pancreas, and gallbladder problems, and fainting. 

Like all drugs in the class, this medication has a black box warning, which is the most serious warning required by the FDA. The warning states that medicines in this drug class have caused thyroid tumors in animal studies. It is not known whether this can occur in humans. However, anyone with medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC) or a family history of MTC, or anyone with Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2) should not take Rybelsus. People who take Rybelsus should be alert to symptoms of thyroid cancer, including a lump in the neck, trouble swallowing or breathing, or hoarseness. 

Are there any Rybelsus-drug interactions?

If you take other medications, Rybelsus interactions may be a concern. Before taking Rybelsus, tell your healthcare provider about all your medications, including prescription and OTC drugs, vitamins, and supplements. 

Drug interactions include:

  • Insulin or certain diabetes medications (glimepiride, glyburide, glipizide) combined with Rybelsus may cause low blood sugar. If the combination is prescribed together, close monitoring of blood sugar levels will be required, and a dose adjustment may be necessary. 

  • Because Rybelsus causes delayed gastric emptying, it can affect the absorption of other medications taken by mouth. Ask your healthcare provider how you need to time your medications.

Is there a cheaper alternative to Rybelsus?

If you are interested in Rybelsus alternatives, you can consult your healthcare provider for medical advice and more information about a change in treatment. Currently, there are no generic alternatives for Rybelsus or any other drug in the GLP-1 agonist class. However, your insurance may charge less for another drug in the class. Or, your healthcare provider may want to try a different medication from another drug class. Your healthcare provider can determine the most appropriate medication based on your medical conditions and medical history. 

How long is the Rybelsus coupon good for?

Rybelsus coupons from SingleCare are unlimited. You can reuse our free Rybelsus savings card on your refills. There are no strict eligibility requirements or limited-time offers.
